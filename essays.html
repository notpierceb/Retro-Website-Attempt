Capitalism and nature
x

https://valleysunderground.files.wordpress.com/2020/04/blackshirts-and-reds-by-michael-parenti.pdf (p.154)

A byproduct of capitalism is a social hierarchy where high-earners are at the top.

This hierarchy is a threatening phenomenae for our planet because for someone in a capitalistic society to fulfill their innate desire to climb the social hierarchy, they must develop an entirely exploitative relationship with nature.

In his essay, Michael Parentis deems the capitalism's "raison d’être" is to "convert nature into commodities and commodities into capital." Essentially transforming the animate earth into inanimate wealth.

Therefore, in order for one to climb capitalism's social hierarchy, one must generate capital at the expense of our finite nature.

"When the last tree has been cut down, the last fish caught, the last river poisoned, only then will we realize that one cannot eat money."

On Consumerism
x

https://sadgrl.online/reading/consumer-ideology

Western advertising is a half-trillion dollar industry dedicated to manipulating the viewer into believing that their lives are lacking.

Advertising sells a way of life, essentially, if you do not own the advertised product, you are not at your happiest.

This cultivates the widespread hysteria that you must always consume more; be more than you currently are.

This constant strife to consume and be "more" is as harmful to the environment as it is the human psyche.
